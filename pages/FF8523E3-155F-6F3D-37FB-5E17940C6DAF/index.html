BENJAMIN'S ARCHIVE AT THE NLI

When Benjamin felt the ground beginning to tremble under his feet he started thinking of the possibility of immigrating to Palestine, as did many other German-Jews at the time. Although he never reached Palestine, he had been sending material to his close friend Gershom Scholem, who was at first head of The Judaica department at the National Library and since 1925 a University professor at the Hebrew University. In fact, Benjamin wrote to Scholem that these materials are all part of the Beniaminia archive which he was slowly building. Benjamin, like Zweig, understood that if he were to perish, the NLI is the only real safe haven for his intellectual work when all else is lost.
