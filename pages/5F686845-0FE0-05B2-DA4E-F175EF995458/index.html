THE HOLOCAUST

One of the most distinct characteristics of the Nazi regime was its belief in the supremacy of the Aryan race and its hatred of Jews and other groups. At the Wannsee Conference in 1942, the Nazi party decided to implement a plan called the “Final Solution of the Jewish Question”. Following the Wannsee Conference, the Jews of Europe were systematically murdered in the extermination camps as part of the Final Solution. In some of the camps permanent gas chambers were erected.

By 1945, 6,000,000 Jews,-the majority of European Jewry- were dead.
