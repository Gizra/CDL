THEODORE HERZL (1860 – 1904)

Theodore (Binyamin Zeev) Herzl was a Jewish journalist from Austro-Hungaria who is considered as the father of the political Zionistic movement.

While serving as a correspondent of the Viennese paper, the Neue Freie Presse in Paris, Herzl witnessed the Dreyfus trial in which a Jewish army officer was unjustly accused of treason. Herzl was horrified upon seeing the mobs in Paris shouting: "Death to the Jews!" This, as well as many other anti-Semitic manifestations, brought him to the conclusion that the Jews must have a state of their own. In 1896

He published the revolutionary book, Der Judenstaat,  and from then on worked relentlessly to promote the idea of a Jewish National State.  Herzl died in 1904 and did not live to see his vision come true.
