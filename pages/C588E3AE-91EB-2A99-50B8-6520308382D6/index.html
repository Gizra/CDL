LETTER BY WALTER BENJAMIN TO GERSHOM SCHOLEM, BERLIN 28 FEBRUARY 1933

In this letter, apparently the last one written by Benjamin to Scholem in Germany, Benjamin has only begun to comprehend the severity of the changes in his homeland. Though he refers to the worsening situation in Germany after the Nazi's takeover in January 1933 as one of his reasons for leaving, he is also occupied with the growing complications involving the publication of his works. Towards the end, he expresses his opinion that Kafka's works are misinterpreted by some of their contemporaries.
