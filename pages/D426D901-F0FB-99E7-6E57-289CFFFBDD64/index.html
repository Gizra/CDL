OTHER MASTERPIECES OF MAIMONIDES
  • THE MISHNEH TORAH

    Following this composition, Maimonides wrote his most famous treatise called the Mishneh Torah, a comprehensive code of Jewish Law which is conceived throughout the Jewish world as one of the chief authoritative codifications of Jewish law and ethics, as well as other important writings. In this great composition Maimonides broke from one thousand years of legal precedent of writing commentaries to produce a systematic codification of Jewish law by subject. Although most of Maimonidesâ€™ treatises were written in Judeo-Arabic, Maimonides composed the Code in Hebrewto ensure its accessibility for Jews all over the world. The manuscript was copied in Spain between 1300-1350, and illustrated around 1400 in Perugia, by the artist Matteo Di Ser Cambio.

  • THE NLI MANUSCRIPT

    Written between 1300-1350 in Spain, it is the most splendid illuminated copy known. Although copied in Spain, most of the illustrations were done in Perugia  Italy around 1400 in the studio of Matteo di Ser Cambio.

  • THE GUIDE OF THE PERPLEXED

    The Guide of the Perplexed, an important philosophical treatise, which was composed between 1185-1191, is set as a letter to a young disciple. The Guide sought to resolve apparent contradictions between rationalist philosophy on the one hand, and Jewish tradition found in Scripture and the Talmud, on the other hand.  Unlike the Mishneh Torah, which was written for broad use, the Guide, written in Judeo-Arabic,  was written for a more limited audience of rationalist yet traditional Jews.
