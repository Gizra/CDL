BENJAMIN'S COMMEMORATIVE ESSAY ON FRANZ KAFKA , 1934

This is Benjamin's personal copy of his essay commemorating Franz Kafka on the occasion of the tenth year after his death, written in exile in 1934. Benjamin was one of the first who dealt intensively with Kafka's work and its Jewish contexts. In this text he also discusses exhaustively a number of famous works by Kafka, among them The Trial. Like others, this typescript with Benjamin's own handwritten corrections, was sent by him to Scholem, who had already been living in Jerusalem for over a decade.
