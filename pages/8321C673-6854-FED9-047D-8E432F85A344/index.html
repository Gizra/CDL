GERMAN-SPEAKING JEWS IN THE NLI ARCHIV

Being integrated into German-speaking society, Jews such as Zweig and Benjamin saw themselves as part of European society, and conceived Jewish identity as a universal phenomenon which lived in harmony with their Europeanism. With the rise of the Nazis to power their world fell apart. Many such Jewish intellectuals including Zweig, Benjamin, the famous poet Elsa Lasker-Schüler , the poet and writer Albert Ehrenstein, and many more, sent their archives to the National Library in Palestine in order to save what they could of their life's achievements and records.
