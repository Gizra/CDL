EXQUISITE MANUSCRIPT COLLECTION
  • JEWISH MANUSCRIPTS

    Yahuda's rich collection of Jewish manuscripts includes the most beautiful existing manuscript of Maimonides' Mishneh Torah, as well as various manuscripts of the Old Testament, commentaries and philological and philosophical treatises written by Jewish scholars.

    Maimonides, Mishne Torah, 1300-1350, 'Mada' (Science), NLI
    Maimonides, Mishne Torah, 1300-1350, NLI
    Maimonides, Mishneh Torah, Judges, copied in 1433, NLI
    Song of Songs, with trans. and Rashi's commentary, 18th century, NLI
  • DOCUMENTS REGARDING THE JEWISH COMMUNITY IN SPAIN

    Being of Spanish origin, Yahuda took a special interest in collecting material pertaining to the history of the Jews in Spain. Among these are documents from the Jewish Community in Aragon, including a privilege granted by King Alfonso V of Aragon (1416-1438) to the nobleman Berenguer de Bardaxi , granting him permission to settle 30-40 Jewish families on his lands in Oliete, south of Zaragoza.

    Privilege granted to the Jews by King Alfonso V of Aragon, Zaragossa 1429, NLI
  • EASTERN MANUSCRIPTS

    Yahuda bequeathed the National Library of Israel with 1,184 exquisite eastern manuscripts in the field of Islamic religion, sciences, Arabic literature and Persian poetry. These include a unique collection of over 100 rare manuscripts of the Qur'an- some of them very early (8th-10th centuries), and others which are beautifully illustrated.

    Qur'an, 9th. century, Qayrawan
    Muhammad al-Busiri, Al-Burdah (poem of the Mantle), mid 14th century, NLI
    An Andalusian Qur'an, 12th century, NLI
  • WESTERN MANUSCRIPTS

    Besides Newton's theological papers, the collection includes beautiful Books of Hours, letters from Napoleon Bonaparte, and many more important manuscripts. 

    Book of Hours, 15th century, Bruges, NLI
    Napoleon's letter to general Destaing during the campaign in Egypt, 9.7.1799
    Newton, Map of the Fifth and Sixth Trumpets, NLI
